# FetchMux founder outreach sequences

**Status:** prepared, not approved for sending  
**Rule:** no message may be sent, automated, posted, or submitted under the owner's identity without
explicit approval and a jurisdiction-appropriate marketing/privacy check.

The goal is ten qualified conversations, not a large contact list. Messages are manual, plain, and
tied to a real public trigger. Do not scrape private contact data, buy a list, evade platform rules,
or pitch inside a project's issue tracker.

## Required evidence before contact

Each candidate record needs:

- name and role from a lawful public source;
- company and relevant product;
- one dated, direct URL showing a retrieval workload, provider integration, reliability issue, or
  active hiring/project need;
- one sentence explaining why that source is relevant;
- jurisdiction/channel review and a suppression check;
- no sensitive inference, private email enrichment, or guessed pain.

If the opening sentence cannot cite a real trigger, remove the lead.

## Sequence A: technical founder with a live AI product

### Message 1

Subject: question about retrieval in `[product]`

> Hi `[first name]`, I saw `[specific public fact]` in `[source]`. I am testing a retrieval gateway
> for small AI teams that already use web search and are considering a second provider for failure
> recovery, cost control, or workload fit.
>
> I am not assuming you have that problem. How does `[product]` handle a slow or rate-limited search
> provider today?
>
> If it is relevant, I would value 20 minutes to understand the workflow. No pitch deck and please
> do not send any keys or private queries.

### Follow-up, four business days later

> Hi `[first name]`, one concrete question to close the loop: when retrieval fails in `[publicly
> named workflow]`, do you retry one provider, switch providers, or let the task fail?
>
> I am looking for real operating behavior, including “this is not a problem.” If it is not on your
> list, I will leave it there.

### Close, seven business days later

> I will close this out. If multi-provider retrieval becomes work later, I can share the private
> pilot outline. Either way, no need to reply.

## Sequence B: AI platform or engineering lead

### Message 1

Subject: how your team measures web retrieval

> Hi `[first name]`, your team publicly described `[specific agent/RAG workflow]` at `[direct URL]`.
> I am researching how platform teams measure retrieval success when provider cost, latency, and
> result quality vary by request.
>
> Do you have a policy metric beyond HTTP success today? I am especially interested in how you
> account for fallback cost and the original request deadline.
>
> I can share the measurement template in exchange for 20 minutes of blunt feedback. This is early
> and I will not claim a provider result I have not measured.

### Follow-up

> One useful answer would be enough: which is harder for your team right now, evaluating providers,
> maintaining the integrations, or explaining failures after the fact?
>
> If none of those is material, I will mark the segment wrong and stop.

## Sequence C: warm introduction request

> Hi `[connector]`, I am looking for one technical founder or AI platform lead who runs a recurring
> web-retrieval workload and has actually considered a second provider. I am not looking for a broad
> AI introduction.
>
> The work is a paid, customer-controlled retrieval economics and reliability audit. It is not
> pooled API credits. Would you be comfortable forwarding the note below to one person? Please do
> not share their details without their agreement.

Forwardable note:

> I am speaking with teams that can show a real retrieval workload and want to measure whether
> provider routing improves reliability, total cost, quality, or engineering time. The first step is
> a 20-minute problem call. No keys or private data are needed.

## Sequence D: reply to an inbound request

> Thanks for reaching out. Before a demo, could you share a non-sensitive description of the
> workload, current provider count, approximate monthly retrieval and downstream context spend,
> engineering time spent on retrieval, the impact of a material failure, and the one metric you
> would want to improve? Please do not send keys, customer queries, or provider output.
>
> If the founding pilot fits, I will send the reviewed scope and secure next steps. Provider usage
> remains on your accounts.

## Public X demand test, draft only

Do not post this until the owner approves the exact text:

> Question for teams running AI agents in production: when web retrieval goes wrong, what actually
> costs you more: provider spend, downstream context tokens, failed tasks, or engineering time?
>
> We are testing a paid retrieval economics audit for teams already using or evaluating two or more
> providers. If this is a real line item rather than an annoyance, reply or DM. No API keys or
> private queries. https://fetchmux.com

This is a buyer-research post, not a launch claim. Measure qualified replies and calls, not views or
likes. Do not boost it with paid spend during the founding test.

## Do not use

- “We can cut your search bill by X%.”
- “We automatically pick the best provider.”
- “We work with” or “we partner with” a named provider.
- “I noticed you struggle with…” when only a job title or generic product page is known.
- fake scarcity, a fake waitlist, invented customers, or an invented benchmark result.
- an AI-written compliment unrelated to the workload.
- a calendar link before the person indicates relevance.

## Reply handling

| Reply | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Not interested / unsubscribe | Confirm suppression if appropriate and do not contact again. |
| Curious but no workload | Share public material only after launch approval; do not consume pilot capacity. |
| Has workload and pain | Run the discovery script; no keys or files yet. |
| Asks for proof | State that customer results do not exist yet; offer the methodology and founding-pilot structure. |
| Asks for free access | Offer no custom work; record whether a self-serve local product would solve the need. |
| Wants pooled credits | Explain BYOK and disqualify if pooled access is required. |
| Wants a provider comparison | Explain that comparative results remain private pending terms and permission. |
